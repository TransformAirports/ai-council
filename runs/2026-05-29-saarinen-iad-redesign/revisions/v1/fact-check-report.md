## Unverified claims

**1. "TRB-typical 12-foot APM platform" (Section VI, Move 3) — NEWLY IDENTIFIED, CORRECTED.**
> Original: "a minimum platform ceiling height set at a design-quality premium above the TRB-typical 12-foot APM platform…"

No Stage 1 brief states a 12-foot platform-ceiling benchmark, and no brief attributes such a figure to TRB. The only TRB citations in the corpus are in the chief-engineer brief, referencing ACRP Report 68 and FAA AC 150/5360-13A — neither of which the draft connects to a 12-foot dimension. The specific number is not load-bearing (the actual argument is "a design-quality premium above the functional minimum"). **Resolution:** replaced with cautious, unsourced-figure-free phrasing in the final draft: "a design-quality premium above the minimum functional clearance typical of transit-grade APM platforms."

**2. Metro modal-split share of IAD ground access (Silver Line section) — ALREADY FLAGGED IN DRAFT.**
> "The current Metro share of IAD ground access [UNVERIFIED — HUMAN REVIEW: Stage 1 briefs did not source a specific percentage…]"

The draft already carries the `[UNVERIFIED — HUMAN REVIEW]` tag with the correct reason. No Stage 1 brief sources a specific Metro/rideshare modal percentage. Tag preserved verbatim in the final draft. The surrounding qualitative claim ("a minority… the overwhelming majority still arrive by car") is consistent with the briefs' framing and is left as cautious phrasing.

**3. The ~84 narrow-body-equivalent gate count — ALREADY SELF-DISCLOSED IN DRAFT.**
> "(The eighty-four-gate figure comes from the revised development strategy and counts the gates the node directly serves… the Stage 1 briefs do not independently source the count.)"

The draft discloses this figure's provenance three times (Exec Summary point 6, Section III §"$22 billion plan read on two axes," and §"The central node, reconsidered"). This is the correct fact-checker handling — the claim is not removed because it is load-bearing to the revision's premise, but its non-Stage-1 origin is transparent to the reader. Preserved as-is.
