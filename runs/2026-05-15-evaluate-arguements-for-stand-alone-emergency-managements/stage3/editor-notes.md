### Voice and rhythm

- The three close-third scenes (DCA 8:47 p.m., AATC tunnel 12/17/2017, FLL 1/6/2017) are preserved verbatim or close to it — they are the pieces that distinguish this from a consultant deck.

## Inline [FC] flags for the Fact-checker

I introduced bracketed inline tags only where my edits either compressed a citation or where the v3 draft already carried a known uncertainty. Three places:

1. **Section II bullet 2 — "the only EMAP-accredited airport in the world."** Tag: `[FC: confirm still current as of 2026]`. The Strategist's claim was anchored to an Airport Improvement 2020 article. If a second airport has accredited in the six years since, the line needs softening.

2. **Section II bullet 3 — "90 percent of county EM directors report to elected officials or to the chief executive directly."** Tag: `[FC: figure is 46% + 44% aggregated from endnote 10 — verify combination]`. The v3 strategist draft does this aggregation in the prose; endnote 10 shows the constituent figures. The combined 90% figure is internally consistent with the source but is the kind of aggregation a hostile reader checks.

3. **Section IV — Yancey County, NC EM director "waited years for additional staff and received them only after Hurricane Helene devastated the county in 2024."** Tag: `[FC: confirm Yancey staffing timeline relative to Helene]`. The ProPublica reporting establishes the case as illustrative of the Argonne pattern; the specific causal sequencing (staff added *after* Helene rather than coincidentally with it) should be confirmed before publication.

4. **Section VI — "Major incidents at DCA reach Congressional staff within hours."** Tag: `[FC: timing softened from v2's "within two hours" — no primary-source anchor per v3 handoff]`. The Strategist's v3 handoff documented this softening explicitly; I preserved the softer phrasing and flagged it again so the Fact-checker can decide whether to soften further or anchor.

## Places I did not cut, and why

- **The DCA-night agency list (Fire, EMS, MPD, USCG, FBI, MD State Police, NTSB, White House Situation Room).** This list is the piece's strongest single demonstration of the coordination thesis — it is the argument made concrete in eight institutional names. A shorter list would weaken it.
- **The eighteen-category §139.325 enumeration ("power failures, hazmat, water rescue, public health events, sabotage, and natural disasters").** Same reason — the rhetorical move is the count, and the count needs the list.
- **Section VII (the FLL close).** Already lean. The "five command posts trying to talk to each other on different radios while the EOC sits in a building no one can enter" is the closing image. Touched only one verb ("was rendered" → "was rendered" remains; one downstream sentence shifted to active voice elsewhere).
- **The hybrid recommendation paragraph in Section VI.** Operational specificity (director-level, duty-officer rotation, convening-authority list, grant strategy, EMAP-as-goal-not-precondition) is the difference between a recommendation that survives a finance and audit committee and a recommendation that does not. Cutting any of it would weaken the proposal's executability.

## Why not deeper than 15%

The target band is 15–25%. I sit on the conservative end at 15.06%. Two reasons:

1. **The v3 strategist draft was already tighter than typical Stage 2 output.** The v2→v3 transition cut ~300 words. The redundancy that justifies a 25% editor pass was largely absent.
2. **Voice cost.** Going past ~15% required cutting either the AATC tunnel scene, the FLL close, or the §139.325 list. Each of those is the piece's voice. The author's brief is "Matt Levine on aviation, not consultant thought leadership" — that voice lives in the specific scenes, not in the analytic connective tissue, and the connective tissue is what I already compressed.

If a deeper cut is wanted, the candidates are: (a) collapse Section II bullet 1 by dropping the four-citation roll (lose ICAO Annex 14, keep §139.325 + SMS + PPD-8), saving ~50 words but weakening the "four regulatory regimes describe enterprise authority" rhetorical move; (b) collapse Section V into a single paragraph rather than five, saving ~80 words but flattening the point-by-point counter-rebuttal structure.

I recommend neither. The Fact-checker pass is the appropriate next step.
