# Fact-Check Report — *From Compliance to Capability: A Strategic Review of the MWAA Contracting Manual*

**Stage 3 verification pass.** Source draft: `outputs/stage3/humanized-draft.md`. Evidence base: the ten Stage 1 briefs in `outputs/stage1/`. Final draft with corrections: `outputs/stage3/final-draft.md`.

**Method.** Every numerical claim, dollar figure, percentage, named example, attributed quotation, and section-cited manual provision in the draft was traced to a supporting Stage 1 brief. The Section VI scoring matrix is explicitly analyst judgment (disclaimed in the draft), so I verified its arithmetic and its factual *anchors* rather than the 1–10 scores themselves.

**Bottom line.** The draft is unusually well-sourced. The overwhelming majority of load-bearing claims trace cleanly to a brief, and the Strategist's own inline `[FACT-CHECK]` tags correctly pre-flag the five thinnest claims (Tampa threshold, DBIA delivery stats, eVA figures, Great Hall cost reconciliation, lease horizon). I found **no fabricated numbers** and **no misattributed quotations**. I made **three corrections** to the final draft: one unverifiable characterization softened, one minor overstatement softened, one dollar-figure misattribution fixed. None is load-bearing to the thesis.

## 2. Unverified claims (flagged individually)

**U-1 — "This was a services buy. No environmental review, no land disturbance, no federal grant concurrence stood between the requirement and the award." (Section I)**
The deep-research brief documents the $4.9M RFP's *timeline* (issued 8/25/2025, closed 12/10/2025, awarded 5/22/2026) but says **nothing** about its subject matter or whether any federal/environmental review applied. The characterization "services buy" with "no environmental review, no land disturbance, no federal grant concurrence" is an inference about a specific contract (RFP-25-25366) that no brief supports.
**Disposition:** Softened in the final draft. The $4.9M value plus a nine-month start-to-finish round trip makes a NEPA-triggering capital action implausible, so I retained the *logic* (an ordinary proposal-based buy where no multi-year federal review sat in the path) but removed the categorical, unverifiable "services buy" assertion and framed the federal-nexus point as evident from the compressed timeline rather than as established fact about that contract.

## 3. Suspected errors (number in draft vs. brief)

**E-1 — "$1.2 million gate re-power" (Section I).**
The airport-coo brief attaches the **$1.2M figure to a curb item** ("a $1.2M curb re-power needs to be done before summer"), not to a gate re-power. Section III of the draft already attaches $1.2M correctly to the curb ("the $1.2 million curb re-strip due before summer peak"). Section I misattributed the figure to "gate re-power."
**Disposition:** Corrected in the final draft — Section I now reads "the gate re-power, the $1.2 million curb re-strip," moving the dollar figure onto the curb item to match the brief and to stay internally consistent with Section III. (Note: these are illustrative operator examples, not real procurements; the carousel motor, gate-area re-power, and curb re-stripe are all supported as example categories in the airport-coo brief.)

## 4. Missing citations / minor overstatements (accurate but loosely sourced)

**M-1 — "more than 130 pages of it" (Section I).**
The technology-scout brief says "Across 130 pages"; deep-research says "over 100 pages." No brief supports a count strictly *greater than* 130. "More than 130" is a mild overstatement.
**Disposition:** Softened to "roughly 130 pages" in the final draft.

**M-2 — "four reforms tie at the top of the matrix at 20" (Section VI intro and Executive summary).**
This is an analyst-scoring framing, not a brief-sourced fact, so it is outside strict fact-check scope. For completeness: the four medium-term/governance items (recs 6, 7, 8, 9) do each score 20, but quick-win **#1 (cycle-time KPI) also scores 20** by the stated formula. The phrase "top of the matrix" is doing rhetorical work — M1 actually scores highest at 21, which the draft acknowledges separately. Internally consistent enough that I left it unchanged, but flagging for human awareness that five items (not four) reach 20 across tiers.
**Disposition:** No change. Scoring is explicitly disclaimed as analyst judgment.
