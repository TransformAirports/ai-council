**Baseline:** 10,223 words (Strategist v3, including ~600 words of Handoff Notes meta-content).
**Output:** 8,530 words.
**Cut:** 16.6% overall (roughly 11% if you exclude the Handoff Notes from the baseline, since they were meta-commentary on the draft rather than the draft itself).

## What I cut and why

1. **Handoff Notes section removed in full (~600 words).** The Strategist's three numbered judgment-call notes to the Editor/Council are valuable orchestration artifacts but are not part of the deliverable. They are decisions about the draft, not the draft. The Coda and the Section VI close already carry the rhetorical weight the Strategist was protecting.

2. **Denver case tightened (~120 words).** Cut the line-item reconciliation footnote on the parallel-system cost figure ($86M vs. $51M discrepancy) — interesting forensics but slowed the narrative and the punch is the $360M total, not the breakdown method.

3. **Section III opening of each canonical case trimmed.** HKK lost the "five days after the first anniversary of the handover" framing (the political-calendar point is made by "calendar the political authorities had set"). T5 lost a redundant "by an airline CEO under oath" qualifier. BER lost a "subsequent" repetition.

4. **Section III closing paragraphs collapsed.** The "Diane Vaughan / normalization of deviance" passage previously restated the four-mode taxonomy twice in adjacent paragraphs. Kept the first instance, cut the second, and removed the "downstream pathway is the place to intervene precisely because…" topic-sentence move — it was scaffolding for an argument the reader had already seen.

5. **Section IV (counter-case) trimmed at arguments 6-9.** Each was strong but loaded with restatement at the end. The taxonomy and incentive objections were the most overwritten — both lost their final summary sentences without losing argument force.

6. **Section V "What is conceded" passage compressed (~150 words).** This section had the most "the implication this carries forward to Section VI is direct" topic sentences. They were doing rhetorical traffic-control and could go without losing substance. The Pittsburgh and EBS concessions in particular got tighter.

7. **Section VI subsections trimmed individually.** The schedule-pressure / bond-documents paragraph lost a redundant "approved program or grant application" → "approved program." The architecture paragraph lost a redundant restatement of the ICS-vs-tilt-tray trade-off (the technical case is already made fully in Section III). The constructability paragraph broke one long compound sentence into two for cadence.

8. **Executive summary claims 2 and 7 lightly trimmed.** Removed parenthetical "operator- and vendor-self-disclosed" duplication and a redundant "covered by prior bonds, PFC, AIP, and BIL/ATP grants" recap that re-appears verbatim in Section VI.

## What I deliberately preserved

- **The full T5 opening anecdote.** Every word in the first three paragraphs of Section I earns its place — the filter, the £4.3B building, the "designed for twenty years" rhythm. This is what the prompt calls "a particular airport on a particular day."
- **The 5:15 a.m. IAD bag-room scene in Section VI.** Same reason. This is the moment a reader stops scanning and starts reading.
- **The eight-claim Executive Summary structure.** The Strategist flagged this in Handoff Notes as a deliberate judgment call about list-form being appropriate here. I agree. The fix was tighter prose inside each claim, not collapsing the structure.
- **Section IV's nine counter-arguments at full strength.** The thesis depends on the counter-case being stated as a real case, not a strawman. Cutting count would weaken the steelman.
- **The Coda intact.** Two paragraphs that do exactly what a coda should do.

## Places I could not fix without Fact-checker input

- The Pittsburgh $1.5B figure (item 3 above) is doing real argumentative work in the Section IV counter-case. If the figure turns out to be unsupported, the counter-argument still stands — but the specificity of "two decades and roughly $1.5 billion" is part of why the calibration warning lands. If the Fact-checker cannot verify, recommend recasting to "roughly two decades of right-sizing capital expenditure" without the dollar figure.
- The Austin-Bergstrom $241.5M reference is a comparison anchor in the Section VI closing. If the figure is unsupported, the comparison structure can stand on the $280–560M envelope alone — but the closing loses rhetorical specificity. Recommend Fact-checker verify before considering removal.
