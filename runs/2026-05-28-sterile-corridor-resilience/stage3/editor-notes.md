## Categories of cut

1. **Restatement-of-the-previous-sentence.** Most common deletion pattern. The Strategist had a tic of landing a paragraph's argument and then re-summarizing it in the next sentence. Examples: cut "This is not a casual observation" (already implied by the literature-review framing); cut "This is the industry's own research program acknowledging..." restatement that immediately followed the ACRP quote (kept the quote and the next analytical move). Removed "The point is not that the corridor has zero buffer. The point is that..." — collapsed to a single sentence carrying the same argument.
2. **"Mission-critical" usage at Xovis.** Removed; Xovis touchpoints are queuing nodes, not load-bearing on the word "mission-critical."
3. **"Holistically"** removed once (handoff geometry "was not designed holistically" → "was not designed as a whole").
4. **"Leverage" (as verb meaning lever)** removed once: "FAA's leverage is funding eligibility" → "FAA's lever is funding eligibility."
5. **Consultant connectives.** Cut "It is also" / "What this means is" / "In other words" instances throughout — the prose now lets the next sentence carry the move rather than announcing it.
6. **Section preambles.** Section IV's opening ("A reader who finds the preceding sections persuasive should also recognize...") trimmed; Section V opening restatement of the contrarian point compressed.
7. **Doubled qualifications.** Where the Strategist added "again" or "again practitioner observation, not a published figure" twice in adjacent paragraphs, kept the first instance fully and shortened the second.
