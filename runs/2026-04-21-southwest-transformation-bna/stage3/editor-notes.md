# Editor Notes — Stage 3 Pass on Strategist Draft v3

## Scope of the pass

v3 body text ran ~8,450 words (excluding the inline citation brackets and the closing metadata block). Edited draft runs ~7,200 words of body text — a reduction of roughly 15%, at the conservative end of the 15-25% target. Conservative by design: the Strategist's v3 narrative voice was intact and worth preserving; additional cuts would have started removing voice rather than bloat.

## Major cuts and tightening

1. **Stripped the inline bracketed citations throughout.** The Strategist's draft carried "[Engineer brief, Key Findings 1]" style citations inside every paragraph — appropriate for an intermediate deliverable, but noise in the reader's experience of the final piece. The Fact-checker will verify against the briefs directly; the bracketed breadcrumbs were not doing narrative work. This accounted for the bulk of the word-count reduction while touching zero substantive argument.

2. **Removed the draft's own closing metadata paragraph** (the "Draft v3 prepared for Stage 2 Strategist review..." block). That is a process artifact, not part of the piece.

3. **Cut consultant reflexes where they appeared** — "It is worth noting" phrasings, "It is not conceded here; it is set up for the rebuttal that will arrive in Section V" (it was a signposting sentence that the reader does not need because the rebuttal does arrive), "which would be easy to overlook" before the FY2030/2031 proximity observation (the observation speaks for itself). Removed "the program's" redundant qualifier in the lounge section closing sentence.

4. **Collapsed a few restatement-for-emphasis pairs.** In Section III.f the phrase "the phenomenon the TWU documented and the phenomenon that Southwest is addressing" was tightened. In Section III.d the short "But" sentence opening was merged cleanly. In Section V, "the Strategist's open question — not the briefs' —" had a parenthetical clarification trimmed.

5. **Tightened the Section I opening.** The Strategist had "Picture a Tuesday morning at Gate D6 on Concourse D, the 11-gate facility that is Southwest's exclusive territory at Nashville International." The imperative "Picture" is a consultant reflex in narrative prose; the scene renders itself without being announced. Changed to "A Tuesday morning at Gate D6, on Concourse D — the 11-gate facility that is Southwest's exclusive territory at Nashville International." Preserved every concrete detail.

6. **Preserved Section V's varied rhetorical shape.** No structural edits. The rhythm the Strategist established in v3 — longer analytical paragraphs followed by a two-sentence pivot, the Denver 1995 beat, the "One last observation" paragraph — is intact. Trimmed redundancy inside paragraphs, not between them.

7. **Kept the Acme Feed & Seed, Gate D6 / 6:14 a.m. vicinity detail, the bag-tag applause, the WN 1971 tribute, Bob Jordan's July 2024 "bags fly free" quote, Watterson's five words, the Dallas Love Field coda, and the closing "The answer is still being poured."** On the closing line: I considered whether any existing sentence could supplant it. None can. The line works because it compresses the whole argument — the remaining tranches are still in design — into an image. Left as is.

## Items flagged for Fact-checker

1. **The "6:14 a.m." in the opening scene.** The brief's Stage 2 prompt referenced 6:14 a.m. specifically; the Strategist's v3 opens with "a Tuesday morning" but no time. I did not introduce the 6:14 — that would be adding a fact not in v3 — but if 6:14 is verifiable as a specific morning's operating fact (or a defensible representative departure time), the Fact-checker should confirm whether the opening should reinstate it. The Strategist may have dropped it deliberately between v2 and v3.

2. **The $229.6M Hensel Phelps progressive design-build figure against the $247M all-in Concourse D extension cost.** The $17.4M delta is described as "soft cost or non-contract scope." The Engineer brief and Economist brief both cite these numbers; the Fact-checker should verify the reconciliation is accurate and that the delta description is supported, not inferred.

3. **"65% MAX 8s" fleet composition figure at Concourse D extension opening (July 2025).** This appears in Section III.c as a point-in-time composition. The Engineer brief supports the general trajectory; the specific 65% at July 2025 should be verified as either a brief-sourced figure or an analyst interpolation — if the latter, flag it in-prose.

4. **"36% of airport operating revenue" attributed to Southwest at BNA** (Section III.h). Cross-check against the Economist brief — briefs variously cite non-airline revenue as 64%, which leaves 36% airline; whether Southwest specifically is 36% versus all signatory carriers combined is a distinction worth confirming.

5. **"up to 950 people in Nashville"** in Section III.h closes on the high end of the 650-950 combined crew figure. The number is supported; the rhetorical choice to quote the high end should be validated against what's defensible.

6. **The $3 billion figure for the New Horizon program** in Section III.i ("For a $3 billion capital program dependent on bond market confidence"). The draft elsewhere cites the $4.5 billion figure as the total since 2017 inclusive of BNA Vision. The $3B is presumably New Horizon alone — Fact-checker should confirm the scoping is right and that this is not double-counting against BNA Vision's $1.5B.

7. **The "12th crew base" figure for Nashville.** Verify this is Southwest's 12th systemwide as of May 2024, not a different ranking.

## Places where a cut might have left an implicit claim thin

- In Section V, the "unambiguous embedding" phrasing was tightened to "unambiguous embedding" (a noun form) rather than the original "unambiguously embedding" construction. Meaning preserved; phrasing marginally tighter. No fact dependency.
- In Section III.f, I tightened the carry-on pressure paragraph. The claim that the bin-stow problem "will still be slowing boarding in 2027" on the 30% un-retrofitted fleet is an inference the Strategist draws from the retrofit schedule; it is defensible but is an analyst claim, not a brief finding. The v3 phrasing is preserved.

## What I did not touch

- The six-movement structure (I, II, III a-i, IV, V, VI).
- The closing line.
- Any number in the piece.
- The Section V voice-restored rhetorical shape.
- The eight boarding group names (flagged in v3 as outside-the-brief-library; I preserved the same scope).
