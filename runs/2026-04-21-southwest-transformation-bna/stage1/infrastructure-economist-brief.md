# Infrastructure Economist Brief: Airport Capital Economics and BNA Vision
## Stage 1 Research — Southwest Transformation at BNA Run
**Prepared by:** Infrastructure Economist Agent

**Word count:** ~2,200

## Key Findings

1. **BNA's aggregate capital program has grown from a $1.2 billion initial budget (2017) to a combined $4.5 billion two-phase commitment ($1.5 billion BNA Vision completed, $3.0 billion New Horizon underway through 2029), representing a cumulative 275% expansion of the original program scope** — before accounting for interest carry on the bond-financed portions. The Bond Buyer reported total New Horizon capital projects (including non-New-Horizon CIPs) at $3.927 billion. [Source: https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/nashville-airport-to-offer-1-28-billion-of-bonds]

2. **Southwest Airlines accounts for approximately 52–54% of BNA enplanements** — the highest carrier-airport concentration among large U.S. hubs outside of legacy-hub structures like Salt Lake (Delta) and Charlotte (American). Southwest operates exclusively from an 11-gate Concourse D and shares Concourse C with American. The airport's next dominant carrier, Delta, holds roughly 12%, followed by American at approximately 10%. [Source: https://www.nashvilleairportbonds.com/fly-nashville-investors-tn/about/i6016]

3. **Senior debt service coverage is projected to decline from 19.11x in FY2024 to 5.95x in FY2025, then fall steadily to 1.96x in FY2029 and 1.80x in FY2030** as the Series 2026 bond issuance ($1.28 billion in senior and subordinate lien bonds, priced January 2026) enters full debt service. Coverage is projected to recover only modestly to 1.88x by FY2032. The contractual floor under the senior bond resolution is 1.25x — leaving a margin of 55 basis points at the 2030 trough under management's base case. [Source: https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/nashville-airport-to-offer-1-28-billion-of-bonds]

4. **Airline cost per enplanement at BNA was $8.49 in FY2024 and $9.16–$9.41 in FY2025 — below the large-hub median — but the authority's own rate-of-return and cost (ROAC) study projects CPE rising from $10.91 in FY2026 to $17.48 by FY2032.** A doubling of CPE over six years, driven almost entirely by debt service, will require airlines — Southwest above all — to absorb materially higher facility costs at Nashville. Whether a carrier mid-transformation accepts that tradeoff is the central financial question the capital plan has not yet explicitly answered. [Source: https://dwuconsulting.com/airports/bna]

5. **The $247 million Concourse D Extension (5 new gates, opened July 2025) and the planned $3 billion New Horizon program are substantially Southwest-exposed investments: Concourse D is Southwest-exclusive, and Southwest represents the primary demand driver for the Concourse A reconstruction (16 gates, targeted July 2028) and the $40 million Central Core Enhancement.** No publicly disclosed agreement binds Southwest to specific gate utilization, departure minimums, or cost-per-use commitments at New Horizon build-out levels. [Sources: https://www.constructiondive.com/news/nashville-airport-concourse-d-extension/753043/; https://flynashville.com/bna-new-horizon]

6. **BNA Vision's final cost was $1.5 billion against an original 2017 announcement of $1.2 billion — a 25% cost escalation over the program's seven-year run.** No public documentation attributes the increase to specific scope changes versus construction inflation versus schedule-related carrying costs; the authority has not characterized the increase as a "cost overrun" and the program was completed in February 2024. The global pattern, illustrated by Berlin Brandenburg (€2.8 billion original estimate, €6.5 billion final cost, 9 years delayed) and the LAX APM ($1.9 billion original, ~$3.5 billion projected final, multiple year delay), is that complex airport construction programs in inflationary environments rarely land at initial estimates. The Concourse D Extension was delivered on time and under its $229.6 million contract — a notable positive precedent within New Horizon. [Sources: https://bnavisionnashville.com; https://www.constructiondive.com/news/nashville-airport-concourse-d-extension/753043/; https://www.airportwatch.org.uk/2020/10/nine-years-late-and-x3-over-budget-due-to-problems-berlins-brandenburg-airport-finally-opens-during-a-pandemic/]

7. **The airport's revenue structure provides meaningful insulation against single-carrier risk: airlines represent only 36% of MNAA operating revenue, with 64% derived from non-airline sources (parking, concessions, ground transportation, rental cars).** Total operating revenue grew from $130 million in FY2021 to $364 million in FY2025. Outstanding debt stood at $2.0 billion as of FY2025 year-end, before the January 2026 bond issuance. Rating agencies — KBRA at AA, Fitch at A+ with positive outlook, S&P at AA- — affirmed the credit in late 2025 but each identified Southwest concentration and multi-year construction risk as the primary credit constraints. [Sources: https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/nashville-airport-to-offer-1-28-billion-of-bonds; https://www.nashvilleairportbonds.com/fly-nashville-investors-tn/about/i6016]

## Evidence Section

### Global and U.S. Airport CapEx Context

Global airport capital expenditure reached $40 billion in 2023, 1% above 2019 levels, according to ACI World data. ACI projects cumulative airport investment of $2.4 trillion through 2040, with $1.7 trillion for brownfield upgrades and $731 billion for new airports. The FAA's 2023–2027 NPIAS identified $62.4 billion in AIP-eligible and justified projects — a 43% increase from the prior two-year cycle. For 2025–2029, the NPIAS estimates $67.5 billion in total eligible capital needs. [Source: https://store.aci.aero/product/global-outlook-of-airport-capital-expenditure/; https://www.faa.gov/airports/planning_capacity/npias]

The FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024 set Airport Improvement Program (AIP) contract authority at $3.35 billion for FY2024, rising to $4.0 billion annually through FY2028. PFC revenue remains capped at $4.50 per eligible enplaned passenger — a cap set in 2000 and not adjusted for inflation despite sustained industry advocacy for an increase to $8.00 or higher. At current volumes (12.4 million enplanements in FY2025), BNA's PFC receipts at the $4.50 cap generate approximately $55.8 million annually, a figure that covers less than 2% of New Horizon's total program cost. [Source: https://www.faa.gov/airports/pfc]

### BNA Capital Program: Scope and Scale

BNA Vision was announced in 2016–2017 with a stated cost of $1.2 billion. [Source: https://www.constructionequipmentguide.com/a-vision-of-the-future-inside-nashville-international-airports-12b-expansion/46846] The program ultimately completed in February 2024 at a reported cost of $1.5 billion — a 25% increase over initial estimates. Projects delivered include a three-phase terminal garage complex, an on-site Hilton-branded hotel, an expanded terminal lobby (the Grand Lobby, opened January 2024), an International Arrivals Facility (opened September 2023), an expanded central checkpoint, additional security screening lanes, and additional airline gates. The program was funded through bonds, federal and state aviation grants, PFC receipts, and other airport funds — without local tax dollars. [Source: https://bnavisionnashville.com/]

New Horizon, the successor program, carries a $3 billion price tag and is expected to deliver by 2029. Combined with BNA Vision, the two-phase total reaches $4.5 billion since 2017. The Bond Buyer and rating agency disclosures identify an all-in capital plan (New Horizon plus other capital improvement projects) of $3.927 billion. [Source: https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/nashville-airport-to-offer-1-28-billion-of-bonds]

New Horizon's major components, per MNAA public disclosures:

- **Concourse D Extension:** $247 million contract (Hensel Phelps / Fentress Architects, progressive design-build). Delivered July 8, 2025, on time and under budget. Adds 5 gates, bringing Concourse D to 11 gates, 161,854 square feet. [Source: https://www.constructiondive.com/news/nashville-airport-concourse-d-extension/753043/]
- **Concourse A Reconstruction:** 16-gate full demolition and rebuild, targeted July 2028. Contract award to Hensel Phelps at CM-at-Risk delivery. Total budget not separately disclosed; Concourse A is the program's largest single project. [Source: https://www.henselphelps.com/project/bna-concourse-d-extension/]
- **Central Ramp Expansion:** 7 overnight and deicing positions; requires 1.1 million cubic yards of fill. Targeted September 2027. [Source: https://flynashville.com/bna-new-horizon]
- **Central Core Enhancement:** $40 million, 18-month program (June 2026 – December 2027). Expanding escalator count from 6 to 16 across floors. [Source: https://hoodline.com/2026/03/bna-braces-for-18-month-shake-up-as-40-million-overhaul-takes-off/]
- **Baggage Handling System:** Three-mile conveyor network with automated sorting. Targeted October 2028. [Source: https://flynashville.com/bna-new-horizon]
- **Rental Car Facility and Parking Garage:** 4,700-vehicle rental car capacity; 3,000-space parking structure. Timeline through 2029. [Source: https://flynashville.com/bna-new-horizon]

Funding sources for New Horizon, per MNAA: bonds, federal and state aviation grants, Passenger Facility Charges, and other airport funds. The Series 2026 bond issuance ($1.28 billion in senior and subordinate lien airport revenue bonds, priced January 8, 2026) is the primary financing vehicle. [Source: https://www.nashvilleairportbonds.com/fly-nashville-investors-tn/about/i6016]

### Southwest Concentration: The Financial Geography

Southwest's 52–54% enplanement share at BNA is the central fact around which BNA's financial structure must be evaluated. Southwest operates exclusively from 11-gate Concourse D (the only single-carrier concourse at BNA) and has significant operations from Concourse C, where it shares space with American Airlines. Delta and United together hold approximately 12% and occupy Concourse B. American holds approximately 10%.

Southwest established BNA as its 12th crew base in 2024, with approximately 500–700 flight attendants and 150–200 pilots based in Nashville. As of 2023–2024, Southwest operated approximately 166 daily departures to 57 nonstop destinations from BNA. The crew base designation is operationally significant: it reflects Southwest's view of BNA as a network anchor, not merely a spoke destination. [Source: https://flynashville.com/news/nashville-international-airport-to-become-new-crew-base-for-southwest-airlines]

The financial implication of this concentration: MNAA's airline cost recovery structure, use-and-lease agreement, and debt service math all rest on assumptions about Southwest's gate utilization, departure frequency, and landing fee payments that will need to hold through a $3 billion construction program that extends to 2029. The bond disclosures note that 80% of BNA's passengers are origin-and-destination rather than connecting — which mitigates some carrier-concentration risk relative to a hub airport — but does not insulate the authority from a scenario in which Southwest's transformation produces network retrenchment, departure banking changes, or gate productivity shifts.

Southwest's lounge investment at BNA is notable in this context. Permit filings from February 2026 document a 30,000 square-foot "Oasis" lounge in the central mezzanine at a fit-out cost of $53 million — the largest in Southwest's emerging lounge pipeline, which also includes Honolulu (12,241 square feet, $20 million minimum) and facilities in Denver, Austin, and Dallas Love Field. [Source: https://hoodline.com/2026/04/southwest-drops-53m-on-swanky-new-bna-lounge-for-music-city-flyers/] A $53 million private investment by the dominant carrier in airport real estate signals ongoing commitment to Nashville as a premium-tier market. It does not, however, constitute an obligation to maintain departure frequency, gate count, or turn-time discipline.

### Debt Structure and Financial Health

MNAA's total outstanding debt as of FY2025 year-end was $2.0 billion. The January 2026 bond issuance (Series 2026A through 2026D) added approximately $1.24 billion in new money, bringing total debt toward $3.2–3.3 billion. [Source: https://www.nashvilleairportbonds.com/fly-nashville-investors-tn/about/i6016]

Debt service coverage trajectory (senior lien):
- FY2024: 19.11x (inflated by $30–40 million in annual federal pandemic relief funds applied to debt service)
- FY2025: 5.95x (last year of meaningful federal relief application, approximately $5.5 million)
- FY2029: 1.96x (projected, base case)
- FY2030: 1.80x (projected trough)
- FY2032: 1.88x (partial recovery as program-period assets generate revenue)

The senior bond resolution requires a minimum 1.25x DSCR. Under management's base case, the minimum margin above covenant is approximately 55 basis points at the FY2030 trough. This is not a crisis scenario — rating agencies accepted it — but it is narrow by historical large-hub standards, and it assumes the revenue projections embedded in the ROAC study hold. [Source: https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/nashville-airport-to-offer-1-28-billion-of-bonds]

The 36%/64% airline-to-non-airline revenue split is a credit positive. It means MNAA's bond coverage does not depend entirely on airline fee payments. But the non-airline revenues (parking, concessions, ground transportation, rental cars) are themselves driven by enplanement volumes — which is itself driven, at BNA, by the carrier that represents 54% of those enplanements.

CPE trajectory from the ROAC study:
- FY2024: $8.49
- FY2025: $9.16–$9.41
- FY2026: ~$10.91 (projected)
- FY2032: ~$17.48 (projected)

A CPE of $17.48 would place BNA near or above the median for comparable large-hub airports. Airlines in general, and Southwest specifically in its current cost-reduction posture (CASM reduction is a stated 2025–2026 priority), will face this as a negotiation at renewal. The current airline use and lease agreement's terms — clarified as part of the Fitch positive-outlook revision in December 2025 — are not publicly detailed in available bond disclosures. [Source: https://dwuconsulting.com/airports/bna]

### Cost Overrun and Schedule Slip: Sector Context

The sector record on major airport capital programs is poor. Key reference cases:

**Berlin Brandenburg Airport (BER):** Original estimate €2.8 billion; final cost approximately €6.5 billion; opened October 2020, nine years behind schedule. Structural design failures in the fire suppression system drove the primary delays. A 2025 academic study documents the business performance impact on the airport authority's owner company. [Source: https://www.airportwatch.org.uk/2020/10/nine-years-late-and-x3-over-budget-due-to-problems-berlins-brandenburg-airport-finally-opens-during-a-pandemic/]

**Denver International Airport Baggage System:** The automated baggage handling system had an original budget of $193 million; final spend reached approximately $311 million. The overall airport opened 16 months late, with cost overruns estimated at $560 million substantially attributable to the baggage system. Total airport cost tripled to $5.2 billion. [Source: https://noonpi.com/the-denver-international-airport-automated-baggage-handling-system/]

**LAX Automated People Mover:** Original estimate $1.9 billion; current projected total approximately $3.5 billion, representing roughly $880 million in overruns. An additional $400 million was approved in 2024 to settle contractor legal claims. No opening date was confirmed as of mid-2025; a 2026 opening is now targeted. [Source: https://laist.com/news/transportation/lax-people-mover-delay-grand-jury-report]

**Heathrow Terminal 5:** A rare counter-example. Budgeted at £4.3 billion (with 20% contingency, approximately £600 million), delivered March 2008 on time and within budget. Attributed to BAA's decision to hold all construction risk on its own balance sheet and use an incentive-structured alliance contract rather than risk transfer to subcontractors. The project team documented that traditional risk transfer arrangements historically produce arbitration, litigation, and delay — conclusions with direct application to BNA's choice of progressive design-build and CM-at-risk delivery methods for its major packages. [Source: https://personalpages.manchester.ac.uk/staff/nuno.gil/Teaching%20case%20studies/BAA%20T5%20Agreement%20.pdf]

BNA Vision's 25% cost escalation ($1.2 billion to $1.5 billion) is modest by sector standards. The Concourse D Extension's on-time, under-budget delivery within New Horizon is a positive early data point. Whether the more complex and larger Concourse A reconstruction ($374,000 square feet, 16 gates) and the baggage handling system can sustain that record through 2028 is unverified and impossible to predict, but program-phase sequential risk is real.

### Carrier Concentration: Historical Precedents

The academic and practitioner record on airports with dominant-carrier concentration offers qualified warnings. Pittsburgh International spent approximately $1 billion (1992 dollars) building a new terminal complex to anchor a US Airways hub. By 2003, US Airways was absorbing an estimated $40 million annual loss from its Pittsburgh hub while paying approximately 80% of the airport's $673 million debt service. Hub collapse followed. [Source: https://crankyflier.com/2012/07/17/across-the-aisle-from-pittsburgh-airports-executive-director-on-pittsburghs-rise-and-fall-as-a-hub-part-1/] Cincinnati-Northern Kentucky (CVG) saw Delta account for approximately 82% of enplanements at peak; CVG was downgraded from Moody's A1 to Baa1 following Delta's de-hubbing in 2013. [Source: https://dwuconsulting.com/dwu-ai/airline-concentration-risk-hub-airports]

The BNA-Southwest situation differs structurally from CVG-Delta or PIT-US Airways. Southwest is not de-hubbing BNA; it established a crew base there in 2024, the inverse signal. The 80% O&D passenger mix means BNA traffic is driven by market demand for Nashville access, not by connecting flows Southwest can reroute. And Southwest's transformation — assigned seating since January 27, 2026; checked bag fees ($45 first bag, $55 second) since late 2025; lounge buildout underway — is a revenue densification strategy, not a network contraction strategy.

But the analogy worth watching is not Pittsburgh or Cincinnati. It is the question of whether capital sized to a carrier's prior operational model — in those cases, hub operations — needs to be reexamined when that operational model changes. At Pittsburgh, the mismatch was between a large connecting-hub terminal and a carrier that stopped connecting passengers. At BNA, the potential mismatch is subtler: infrastructure designed for a carrier that turned aircraft in 25 minutes, filled every seat on the same fare basis, and required minimal gate-area amenity, now serving a carrier that is deliberately slowing its operational cadence (assigned boarding, premium zone boarding, lounge queuing, checked bag processing) and investing in premium gate-area experiences. Those are not the same gate-area requirements, and they are not the same turn-time profiles.

### Opportunity Cost

At $4.5 billion committed since 2017, BNA's capital program is approximately:
- 41x BNA's FY2021 total operating revenue ($130 million)
- 12.4x BNA's FY2025 total operating revenue ($364 million)
- Equivalent to roughly $365 per enplanement at BNA's current 12.4 million annual volume — or $113 per enplanement against a 40-million-passenger future that is not yet realized

The opportunity cost argument is not that BNA should have skipped terminal expansion — Nashville's 34.9% enplanement growth from 2019 to 2024 (to 12.06 million enplanements) made capacity investment necessary. The argument is about timing, staging, and the assumptions embedded in each committed phase. The Central Core Enhancement ($40 million, underway June 2026), the Concourse A reconstruction (ongoing), and the baggage handling system rebuild (targeted October 2028) each reflect a specific demand forecast and, implicitly, a specific assumption about Southwest's operational footprint. None of those projects are easily reversible once committed.

ACI-NA's estimate puts U.S. airport infrastructure investment needs at $173.9 billion over five years. At BNA, the question is whether the $3 billion New Horizon program's uncommitted tranches have been stress-tested against the post-transformation Southwest — a carrier whose operational assumptions, not its presence at BNA, have changed materially.

## Caveats and Limitations

**PDF inaccessibility.** The primary MNAA financial documents — the FY2025 ACFR (available at flynashville.com) and the FY2024 ACFR — returned as image-encoded PDFs that could not be parsed for text extraction. CPE figures, revenue breakdowns, and debt schedules quoted in this brief are drawn from Bond Buyer reporting, KBRA and Fitch press releases, BondLink investor disclosures, and the DWU Consulting airport finance database. These are credible secondary sources, but the primary source documents should be consulted for precision.

**Southwest concentration percentage.** The 52–54% figure appears across multiple sources (Bond Buyer, Hoodline, UpgradedPoints) but derives ultimately from a single MNAA disclosure to rating agencies. BTS market share data by originating carrier at BNA would provide a more granular and independently verifiable figure; this data was not pulled for this brief.

**New Horizon phase-specific costs.** The aggregate New Horizon budget is publicly stated at $3 billion. Individual project budgets — Concourse A reconstruction in particular — are not separately disclosed in available public documents. The $229.6 million Concourse D contract value (from Hensel Phelps' project sheet) is the only publicly confirmed project-level budget for New Horizon.

**Airline use and lease agreement terms.** Fitch's December 2025 rating revision cited "clarification of a previously adopted airline use and lease agreement" as a positive credit development, but the agreement's specific terms — cost recovery methodology, rate-setting provisions, gate-use minimums — are not publicly disclosed in the documents reviewed. This is a material gap for understanding how BNA's CPE trajectory will affect Southwest's total facility cost at Nashville.

**BNA Vision cost escalation documentation.** The $1.2 billion to $1.5 billion increase is documented by comparing the 2017 announcement with current references to program cost, but no MNAA document reviewed explicitly characterizes this as a scope change versus a cost overrun versus construction inflation. The authority has not labeled it an overrun. The distinction matters for capital planning lessons.

**Schedule slip documentation.** No specific schedule-slip events within BNA Vision were documented in available public sources. The program completed in February 2024; the original 2017 announcement did not specify a completion date with precision. Within New Horizon, the Concourse D Extension was delivered on time per CEO Doug Kreulen's statement at opening.
