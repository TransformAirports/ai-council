# Editor's notes — Stage 3 edit of "The Cheapest Lever"

## Word count

- Strategist v3: 10,799 words
- Editor's cut: 9,177 words
- **Net reduction: 1,622 words (15.0%)**

Within the 15–25% target band, at the low end. The argument was already dense; further cuts would have started shaving evidence rather than restatement.

## Major changes

### Where the cuts came from

1. **Section II (Executive Summary).** Heaviest pass. The summary was running ~900 words and rehearsing Section III's evidence in detail (Passini, ACRP 52 numbers, Pittsburgh, IAD/DCA construction eras). Trimmed to a true summary that previews the argument and the two execution constraints without re-arguing them. The Pittsburgh paragraph compressed from ~150 words to ~80; the methodology paragraph dropped the 47/56 meter spacing numbers (still in Section III).

2. **Section V (Counter-Case Insufficient).** Lots of restatement of Section IV's structure plus internal repetition. Cut transitional sentences like "The honest concession follows," collapsed two paragraphs on the Saarinen / Mobile Lounge mismatch into one. Dropped redundant qualifiers on the international exemplars (already named in IV).

3. **Section III (Argument), Eye-Tracking subsection.** Tightened the Heathrow/Munich/2025-lab narrative without losing any of the three studies. Cut the "implication is precise" lead-in to the filter-generalization point — the next sentence makes the case directly.

4. **Section III, "Why Digital Is Not the Answer."** Compressed the FIDS detail and Apple Maps installed-by-default restatement.

5. **Section VI (Implications for MWAA).** Tightened the cost-derivation paragraph (still order-of-magnitude with full math chain). Trimmed the "named owner" subsection's restatement of the volume-count point.

### Voice preserved deliberately

- Section I's IAD opening scene is intact. It is the hook and carries the concrete specificity (United blue, Saarinen 1962, Mobile Lounge cadence) the rest of the report references.
- The short single-sentence paragraphs ("The standard exists." / "Two decades of mismatch." / "Those are MWAA's.") are kept for rhetorical weight.
- Mollerup's five-word Manchester quote retained verbatim.
- Closing paragraphs (the connecting passenger returning to the opening) untouched — they close the loop with the report's narrative architecture.
- Aside on AC 150/5340-18H vs. 150/5360-12F kept; it's the kind of "pointed aside" the run prompt asked to preserve and it inoculates the report against a common scope confusion.

### Buzzwords killed

- "highest-leverage operational intervention" → "highest-impact operational intervention" (Section I thesis statement). The CLAUDE.md ban on "leverage" applies; the noun "lever" in the title is retained because it is the operative metaphor.
- "material airline leverage" → "the carriers hold substantial power" (Section V).
- No other instances of "leverage," "synergy," "holistic," "best-in-class," "paradigm shift," "ecosystem," "absolutely," "game-changer," or "mission-critical" were found in the v3 draft. The Strategist had largely complied.

### Consultant reflexes removed

- "The Architectural Historian's underlying observation makes the point harder still" → cut the connective; the observation lands on its own.
- "The honest concession follows" → cut and replaced with direct statement.
- "This dynamic explains why..." → kept (it actually does explanatory work, not just transitional throat-clearing).
- Section I closing was lightly reworked: "This report argues three things" was removed because only one bold thesis followed — the framing oversold the count.
- "What this is not" closer in Section VI is kept; it does real argumentative work (heading off the typeface-recommendation reading).

### Transitions tightened

- Cut several "This section will..." style topic sentences. The prose now leads from one section to the next through argument rather than announcement.

## Fact-checker flags

Three numerical or attributed claims I could not verify against the cited briefs and have tagged inline with `[FACT-CHECK: ...]`. The Fact-checker should resolve these against the Stage 1 briefs and either confirm, soften, or strike:

1. **`[FACT-CHECK: D-gate misconnect rate]`** (Handoff notes). The Strategist's own handoff notes flagged that the brief does not give a misconnect probability for D-gate connections at IAD. The opening scene is framed with "Imagine" to handle this; the flag is for transparency, not removal.

2. **`[FACT-CHECK: 80% directional-question share]`** (Section III, "The Decision Point Is the Unit of Analysis"). The Strategist labels the figure as vendor-sourced, directional, not audited — and the text already treats it as a proxy rather than a measurement. Worth verifying the Operations Analyst brief actually contains the "roughly 80 percent" figure and not a different number.

3. **`[FACT-CHECK: 35–40% beyond-lifecycle figure]`** (Section III, "Why Digital Is Not the Answer"). The "estimated 35 to 40 percent of FIDS units currently installed at US commercial airports are operating beyond their stated lifecycle" claim is sourced to the Chief Engineer brief. Verify the brief contains this range and not a different one.

4. **`[FACT-CHECK: 6–9x ten-year return calculation]`** (Section V, asymmetry paragraph). The Strategist asserts a "six to nine times program cost" return on the revenue line over ten years. The math chain to that figure is not laid out in the draft and may not be in the briefs as a direct claim — could be an analyst calculation that needs its derivation shown or its range narrowed.

## Items I did not change

- The Pittsburgh case detail in Section III. The Strategist made it the empirical anchor of the contradiction-debt argument; it is the longest single example and earns the space because the parallel to MWAA's situation is the load-bearing analogy of the report.
- The $700M figure in Section V. Per the Strategist's handoff note, retaining it once with explicit dismissal is more useful than silent absence. Kept.
- The Saarinen catenary-cable roof discussion in Section VI. Preservation review and historic register details are the specific operational constraint that distinguishes IAD from a generic terminal.
- The two-airport breakout (IAD six eras / DCA three eras) appears once in Section III. The Executive Summary's earlier IAD construction-era enumeration was cut to avoid duplication.

## Net assessment

The edit removes restatement and tightens sentences without surgically altering the argument structure (six sections, the same thesis chain, the same evidence). Voice and cadence are preserved; the report still reads like a long-form essay rather than a consultant deck. The four fact-check flags above are the only places where the editor's pass deferred judgment to the next stage.
