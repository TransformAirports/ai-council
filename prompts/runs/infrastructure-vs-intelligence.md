# Run: Infrastructure vs. Operational Intelligence

## Thesis

Airports are over-investing in infrastructure and under-investing in operational intelligence. The next billion dollars of CapEx at major US hubs should be pressure-tested against a much smaller bet on software, data, and coordination systems that make existing assets meaningfully more productive.

## Audience

MWAA leadership, airport planners across the industry, and policy readers. Assume sophistication. Assume skepticism. Assume they've read McKinsey decks and are tired of them.

## Tone

Direct. Evidence-dense. Intellectually honest about the counter-argument. Slightly provocative but not polemical. Think Matt Levine on aviation, not a consultant thought leadership piece.

## Length

Final document: 8,000-10,000 words. This is a substantive piece, not a blog post.

## What this is NOT

- A vendor pitch for any specific technology
- A criticism of MWAA's capital program specifically (use MWAA examples where relevant but the argument is industry-wide)
- A call for zero infrastructure investment (that position is indefensible)
- A piece that ignores the genuine reasons airports build things

## What this IS

- A rigorous argument that the *ratio* of infrastructure to operational intelligence investment is wrong
- A piece that forces engaged planners to defend their capital programs on new ground
- A demonstration that the counter-argument has been taken seriously and still loses on the specifics
